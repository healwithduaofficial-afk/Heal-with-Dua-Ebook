Guide
How to Use This Book

Welcome to Heal with Dua — a sanctuary for your heart. Here is how to journey through these pages:

  1. Identify Your Emotion
    When feelings arise, turn to the Index and find the chapter that resonates with your current state. Don't suppress what you feel — bring it to Allah.
  2. Read the Introduction
    Each chapter begins with a gentle introduction to help you understand your emotion through an Islamic lens. No feeling is "bad"; each is an invitation to turn to Him.
  3. Recite the Dua
    Read the Arabic script slowly, even if you don't understand every word. Then read the transliteration to help with pronunciation. Finally, read the meaning with presence of heart.
  4. Learn the Origin Story
    Understanding where a dua comes from — which Prophet or Companion spoke these words, and in what circumstance — helps us feel less alone in our struggle.
  5. Reflect and Write
    Use the reflection prompts and writing spaces to pour out your heart. This is your private conversation with Allah and with yourself.
  6. Track Your Journey
    Use the Monthly Emotion Tracker at the end of the book to notice patterns, growth, and the mercy of Allah in your life over time.
  7. Share (Optional)
    If you find benefit, share your reflections or favorite duas with others using @heal.with.dua — you never know whose heart you might heal.

May this book become a companion in your darkest nights and your brightest mornings.

This book gathers the most beautiful, authentic duas from the Quran and Sunnah — the very words spoken by Prophets when they were in the belly of a whale, in the depths of illness, in the grip of fear, and in moments of overwhelming gratitude. Whether you are anxious, grieving, angry, lonely, hopeful, grateful, or struggling to keep faith — there is a dua here for you. There is a story that mirrors yours. There is an ayah that was revealed to comfort a heart just like yours.

May this book become a companion in your darkest nights and your brightest mornings. May it remind you that no emotion is too small for Allah, and no dua is ever wasted.

With love and prayers,

Chapter One
Anxiety & Worry
When the heart races and the mind won't rest

Introduction

Anxiety is not a sign of weak faith — it is a sign of a heart that cares, that hopes, that fears loss. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ himself experienced moments of concern, and he taught us specific words to soothe the restless heart. In Islam, anxiety is an invitation to tawakkul (trust in Allah), not a reason for self-blame.

Allah says: "Indeed, Allah is with those who fear Him and those who are doers of good." (Surah An-Nahl 16:128). Your worry is seen. Your dua is heard.

The Dua for Anxiety & Sorrow

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْهَمِّ وَالْحُزْنِ، وَالْعَجْزِ وَالْكَسَلِ، وَالْبُخْلِ وَالْجُبْنِ، وَضَلَعِ الدَّيْنِ وَغَلَبَةِ الرِّجَالِ
Allahumma inni a'udhu bika minal-hammi wal-huzni, wal-'ajzi wal-kasali, wal-bukhli wal-jubni, wa dala'id-dayni wa ghalabatir-rijal.
"O Allah, I take refuge in You from anxiety and sorrow, weakness and laziness, miserliness and cowardice, the burden of debts and from being overpowered by men."
Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari

The Comprehensive Dua of the Prophet ﷺ

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي عَبْدُكَ، ابْنُ عَبْدِكَ، ابْنُ أَمَتِكَ، نَاصِيَتِي بِيَدِكَ، مَاضٍ فِيَّ حُكْمُكَ، عَدْلٌ فِيَّ قَضَاؤُكَ، أَسْأَلُكَ بِكُلِّ اسْمٍ هُوَ لَكَ، سَمَّيْتَ بِهِ نَفْسَكَ، أَوْ أَنْزَلْتَهُ فِي كِتَابِكَ، أَوْ عَلَّمْتَهُ أَحَدًا مِنْ خَلْقِكَ، أَوِ اسْتَأْثَرْتَ بِهِ فِي عِلْمِ الْغَيْبِ عِنْدَكَ، أَنْ تَجْعَلَ الْقُرْآنَ رَبِيعَ قَلْبِي، وَنُورَ صَدْرِي، وَجَلَاءَ حُزْنِي، وَذَهَابَ هَمِّي
Allahumma inni 'abduka, ibnu 'abdika, ibnu amatika, nasiyati biyadika, madin fiyya hukmuka, 'adlun fiyya qada'uka, as'aluka bi kulli ismin huwa laka, sammaita bihi nafsaka, aw anzaltahu fi kitabika, aw 'allamtahu ahadan min khalqika, aw ista'tharta bihi fi 'ilmi al-ghaybi 'indaka, an taj'alal-Qurana rabee'a qalbi, wa nura sadri, wa jala'a huzni, wa dhahaba hammi.
"O Allah, I am Your servant, son of Your servant, son of Your maidservant. My forelock is in Your hand, Your command over me is forever executed and Your decree over me is just. I ask You by every Name belonging to You which You named Yourself with, or revealed in Your Book, or You taught to any of Your creation, or You have preserved in the knowledge of the unseen with You, that You make the Quran the spring of my heart, and the light of my chest, the banisher of my sadness and the reliever of my anxiety."
Reference: Ahmad ibn Hanbal

Origin Story

These duas were frequently recited by the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and taught to his companions. The first dua addresses the many faces of anxiety — worry, grief, helplessness, laziness, financial stress, and feeling overpowered. The Prophet ﷺ would seek refuge from all of these together, teaching us that anxiety is multifaceted and that Allah's protection covers every form.

The second, longer dua is one of the most comprehensive supplications in Islamic tradition. It emphasizes our complete dependence on Allah — even our very existence is in His hand. The Prophet ﷺ promised that whoever recites this with sincerity, Allah will replace their anxiety with tranquility.

Allah does not burden a soul beyond that it can bear. It will have [the consequence of] what [good] it has gained, and it will bear [the consequence of] what [evil] it has earned. Our Lord, do not impose blame upon us if we have forgotten or erred. Surah Al-Baqarah 2:286

  1. What is weighing on my heart right now? Be specific.
  2. What would it feel like to truly believe that Allah is handling this affair?
  3. What is one small step I can take today while trusting Allah with the outcome?
حَسْبِيَ اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ عَلَيْهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ وَهُوَ رَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِيمِ
"Allah is sufficient for me. There is no deity except Him. In Him I have placed my trust, and He is the Lord of the Magnificent Throne."
Reference: Surah At-Tawbah 9:129
Chapter Two
Sadness & Grief
When the heart weeps and the world feels heavy

Introduction

Grief is the price we pay for love. In Islam, tears are not signs of weakness — they are signs of a soft heart. The Prophet ﷺ wept when his son Ibrahim passed away, and when asked, he said: "The eyes shed tears and the heart grieves, but we do not say anything except what pleases our Lord."

Your sadness is valid. Your tears are witnessed. And your dua for relief is beloved to Allah.

The Dua of Prophet Yunus (AS)

لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ سُبْحَانَكَ إِنِّي كُنْتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ
La ilaha illa anta subhanaka inni kuntu minaz-zalimin.
"There is no deity except You; exalted are You. Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers."
Reference: Surah Al-Anbiya 21:87

The Dua of Prophet Ayyub (AS)

أَنِّي مَسَّنِيَ الضُّرُّ وَأَنْتَ أَرْحَمُ الرَّاحِمِينَ
Annee massaniyad-durru wa Anta Arhamur-Rahimin.
"Indeed, distress has touched me, and You are the Most Merciful of the merciful."
Reference: Surah Al-Anbiya 21:83

Origin Story

Prophet Yunus (AS) left his people in anger when they rejected his message, thinking Allah would not constrain him. A whale swallowed him whole, and he found himself in three layers of darkness: the night, the ocean depths, and the belly of the whale. In that utter isolation, he uttered the most sincere repentance. Allah tells us: "So We responded to him and saved him from the distress. And thus do We save the believers." (21:88). The Prophet ﷺ said: "No Muslim supplicates with it for anything, ever, except Allah responds to him." (Tirmidhi)

Prophet Ayyub (AS) was tested with the loss of wealth, children, and health for years — some narrations say eighteen years. His body was covered in sores, yet his wife remained by his side. He never complained of injustice. He simply said: "Distress has touched me, and You are the Most Merciful." His dua was answered with double restoration — new family, renewed health, and multiplied blessings.

For indeed, with hardship [will be] ease. Indeed, with hardship [will be] ease. Surah Ash-Sharh 94:5-6

Reflect & Write

  1. What have I lost, and what did it mean to me? Name it. Honor it.
  2. How can I follow the example of Prophet Ayyub (AS) — acknowledging pain while affirming mercy?
  3. What is one thing Allah has not taken from me that I can be grateful for today?
اللَّهُمَّ رَحْمَتَكَ أَرْجُو فَلَا تَكِلْنِي إِلَىٰ نَفْسِي طَرْفَةَ عَيْنٍ وَأَصْلِحْ لِي شَأْنِي كُلَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
"O Allah, it is Your mercy that I hope for, so do not leave me in charge of my affairs even for a blink of an eye, and rectify for me all of my affairs. None has the right to be worshipped except You."
Reference: Abu Dawud
Chapter Three
Anger & Frustration
When the fire rises and reason fades

Introduction

Anger is a natural emotion, but in Islam, it is compared to a burning coal in the hand — it harms the one who holds it first. The Prophet ﷺ said: "The strong is not the one who overcomes people by his strength, but the strong is the one who controls himself while in anger." (Bukhari)

This chapter gives you the tools to extinguish that fire before it burns your relationships, your peace, and your faith.

The Dua for Anger

أَعُوذُ بِاللَّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ
A'udhu billahi minash-shaytanir-rajim.
"I seek refuge in Allah from the accursed devil."
Reference: Surah Al-Mu'minun 23:97 & Sunnah

The Dua to Remove Anger from the Heart

اللَّهُمَّ أَذْهِبْ غَيْظَ قَلْبِي
Allahumma adhhib gaydha qalbi.
"O Allah, remove anger from my heart."
Reference: Ibn Majah

Origin Story

The Prophet ﷺ taught us that anger comes from Shaytan, who was created from fire. When a person gets angry, Shaytan whispers destructive thoughts. The Prophet ﷺ advised: "If one of you is angry while standing, let him sit down. If his anger leaves him, fine; otherwise, let him lie down." (Abu Dawud)

Reciting "A'udhu billahi minash-shaytanir-rajim" creates an immediate spiritual barrier. It reminds us that our anger is not our true self — our true self is the calm, rational believer who chooses mercy over revenge.

Who spend [in the cause of Allah] during ease and hardship and who restrain anger and who pardon the people — and Allah loves the doers of good. Surah Aal-E-Imran 3:134

Reflect & Write

  1. What triggered my anger? Was it the situation, or something deeper (fear, hurt, injustice)?
  2. How did the Prophet ﷺ handle anger in this type of situation?
  3. What would 'restraining anger' look like in my specific circumstance?
اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي ذَنْبِي، وَأَذْهِبْ غَيْظَ قَلْبِي، وَأَعِذْنِي مِنْ مُضِلَّاتِ الْفِتَنِ
"O Allah, forgive my sin, remove anger from my heart, and protect me from misleading trials."
Reference: Abu Dawud
Chapter Four
Fear & Insecurity
When the world feels too big and I feel too small

Introduction

Fear is a biological alarm system, but when it becomes chronic, it paralyzes. The Quran is filled with the command: "Do not fear, and do not grieve." — mentioned over a dozen times. Why? Because the antidote to fear is not courage; it is certainty in Allah's protection.

Whether you fear people, the future, failure, or harm — these duas armor your heart with divine confidence.

The Dua for Fear & Apprehension

أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّاتِ مِنْ غَضَبِهِ وَعِقَابِهِ، وَشَرِّ عِبَادِهِ، وَمِنْ هَمَزَاتِ الشَّيَاطِينِ وَأَنْ يَحْضُرُونِ
A'udhu bikalimatillahi at-tammati min ghadabihi wa 'iqabihi, wa sharri 'ibadihi, wa min hamazatish-shayateeni wa an yahdurun.
"I take refuge in the perfect words of Allah from His anger and punishment, from the evil of His servants, and from the whispers of devils and their presence."
Reference: Sahih Muslim

The Dua of Protection

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الَّذِي لَا يَضُرُّ مَعَ اسْمِهِ شَيْءٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا فِي السَّمَاءِ، وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
Bismillahil-ladhi la yadurru ma'asmihi shai'un fil-ardi wa la fis-sama'i, wa Huwas-Sami'ul-'Alim.
"In the name of Allah with Whose name nothing is harmed on earth nor in the heavens, and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing."
Reference: Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi

Origin Story

The Prophet ﷺ taught these duas to his grandsons Hasan and Husayn, and to his daughter Fatimah (RA) for morning and evening protection. The second dua is so powerful that the Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever says it three times in the morning and evening, nothing will harm him until he reaches the evening/morning."

These words are not magic — they are declarations of trust. When you say "In the name of Allah," you are placing an invisible shield around your soul, acknowledging that no harm can reach you except what Allah has decreed, and what He decrees is always for your ultimate good.

And whoever fears Allah — He will make for him a way out, and will provide for him from where he does not expect. Surah At-Talaq 65:2-3

Reflect & Write

  1. What am I most afraid of right now? Name it to tame it.
  2. How does knowing 'Allah is sufficient for me' change my perspective on this fear?
  3. What would I do differently today if I truly believed Allah is protecting me?
اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّا نَجْعَلُكَ فِي نُحُورِهِمْ وَنَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شُرُورِهِمْ
"O Allah, we place You before them and we take refuge in You from their evil."
Reference: Abu Dawud
Chapter Five
Hope & Optimism
When dawn breaks after the longest night

Introduction

Hope in Islam is not naive positivity — it is active trust. It is planting seeds while trusting the rain will come. It is the Prophet ﷺ leaving Makkah after years of persecution, knowing Allah would open Makkah's hearts. It is the migrant leaving everything behind, believing Allah will replace with better.

This chapter is for the moments when you need to remember: Allah's mercy is bigger than your mistakes, and His plan is wider than your vision.

The Dua for Ease in Affairs

اللَّهُمَّ لَا سَهْلَ إِلَّا مَا جَعَلْتَهُ سَهْلًا، وَأَنْتَ تَجْعَلُ الْحَزْنَ إِذَا شِئْتَ سَهْلًا
Allahumma la sahla illa ma ja'altahu sahlan, wa Anta taj'alul-hazna idha shi'ta sahlan.
"O Allah, there is no ease except in that which You have made easy, and You make the difficulty, if You wish, easy."
Reference: Ibn Hibban

The Dua of Prophet Musa (AS)

رَبِّ إِنِّي لِمَا أَنْزَلْتَ إِلَيَّ مِنْ خَيْرٍ فَقِيرٌ
Rabbi inni lima anzalta ilayya min khayrin faqir.
"My Lord, indeed I am, for whatever good You would send down to me, in need."
Reference: Surah Al-Qasas 28:24

Origin Story

Prophet Musa (AS) fled Egypt after accidentally killing a man. He arrived in Madyan exhausted, thirsty, and alone — a fugitive with nothing. He helped two women water their sheep at a well, then sat under a tree in utter desperation. He didn't ask for wealth, power, or revenge. He simply said: "I am in need of whatever good You send down."

Allah answered with a home, a family, and a mission that would change history. This dua teaches us that admitting our poverty before Allah is the first step to receiving His abundance.

The first dua reminds us that ease is not found in circumstances — it is found in Allah's decree. What seems impossible to us is simple for Him.

And whoever fears Allah — He will make for him a way out, and will provide for him from where he does not expect. And whoever relies upon Allah — then He is sufficient for him. Surah At-Talaq 65:2-3

Reflect & Write

  1. What 'impossible' situation am I facing that I need Allah to make easy?
  2. How did Prophet Musa's dua change his life? What good might Allah be preparing for me?
  3. What would I attempt today if I truly believed Allah would provide?
يَا حَيُّ يَا قَيُّومُ بِرَحْمَتِكَ أَسْتَغِيثُ
"O Ever-Living, O Sustainer, by Your mercy I seek relief."
Reference: Tirmidhi
Chapter Six
Gratitude & Contentment
When the heart overflows with thankfulness

Introduction

Gratitude is the currency of the believer. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever is not grateful to people is not grateful to Allah." But gratitude to Allah comes first — it is recognizing that every breath, every bite of food, every moment of safety is a gift we did not earn.

This chapter is not just for when things go well. It is for training the heart to see blessings in disguise, to say Alhamdulillah in all states, and to find richness in what we already have.

The Dua for Gratitude

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ زَوَالِ نِعْمَتِكَ، وَتَحَوُّلِ عَافِيَتِكَ، وَفُجَاءَةِ نِقْمَتِكَ، وَجَمِيعِ سَخَطِكَ
Allahumma inni a'udhu bika min zawali ni'matika, wa tahawwuli 'afiyatika, wa fuja'ati niqmatika, wa jami'i sakhati.
"O Allah, I seek refuge in You from the decline of Your blessings, the passing of safety, the sudden onset of Your punishment, and from all that displeases You."
Reference: Sahih Muslim

The Dua of Praise & Gratitude

اللَّهُمَّ لَكَ الْحَمْدُ كَمَا يَنْبَغِي لِجَلَالِ وَجْهِكَ وَعَظِيمِ سُلْطَانِكَ
Allahumma laka al-hamdu kama yanbaghi li jalali wajhika wa 'azimi sultanik.
"O Allah, to You is praise as befits the majesty of Your Face and the greatness of Your Sovereignty."
Reference: Sahih Muslim

Origin Story

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Shall I not tell you about the best of deeds, the purest in the sight of your Lord, about the one that is of the greatest benefit to you, that raises your position the most, and that is better for you than spending gold and silver?" They said: "Of course." He said: "The remembrance of Allah." (Tirmidhi)

This dua elevates gratitude beyond words — it acknowledges that no human praise can fully match Allah's majesty, yet we strive to offer what befits Him. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Allah is pleased with a servant who eats and praises Him, and drinks and praises Him." (Muslim)

The first dua teaches us that gratitude includes asking Allah to preserve blessings, not just receive them. It is the dua of someone who recognizes how fragile safety and health truly are.

And [remember] when your Lord proclaimed, 'If you are grateful, I will surely increase you [in favor]; but if you deny, indeed, My punishment is severe.' Surah Ibrahim 14:7

  2. What difficulty in my past turned out to be a hidden blessing?
  3. How can I show gratitude through action today, not just words?
اللَّهُمَّ أَعِنِّي عَلَىٰ ذِكْرِكَ وَشُكْرِكَ وَحُسْنِ عِبَادَتِكَ
"O Allah, help me to remember You, to thank You, and to worship You in the best manner."
Reference: Abu Dawud, Nasa'i
Chapter Seven
Loneliness & Isolation
When the world feels empty and the phone stays silent

Introduction

Loneliness is one of the most painful human experiences. But in Islam, solitude can become khalwah — a sacred space where you are alone with Allah. The Prophet ﷺ would retreat to the Cave of Hira before revelation came. Many great moments in Islamic history happened in isolation: the Prophet's night prayers, Maryam's labor under the palm tree, Yunus's transformation in the whale.

This chapter reminds you: you are never truly alone. Allah is closer than your jugular vein.

The Dua of Prophet Sulayman (AS)

وَقُلْ رَبِّ أَوْزِعْنِي أَنْ أَشْكُرَ نِعْمَتَكَ الَّتِي أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيَّ وَعَلَىٰ وَالِدَيَّ وَأَنْ أَعْمَلَ صَالِحًا تَرْضَاهُ وَأَصْلِحْ لِي فِي ذُرِّيَّتِي إِنِّي تُبْتُ إِلَيْكَ وَإِنِّي مِنَ الْمُسْلِمِينَ
Wa qul rabbi awzi'ni an ashkura ni'matikal-lati an'amta 'alayya wa 'ala walidayya wa an a'mala salihan tardahu wa aslih li fi dhurriyyati inni tubtu ilayka wa inni minal-muslimin.
"My Lord, enable me to be grateful for Your favor which You have bestowed upon me and upon my parents, and to do righteousness of which You approve, and admit me by Your mercy among Your righteous servants."
Reference: Surah An-Naml 27:19

The Dua for Companionship

وَاجْعَلْنِي لِلْمُتَّقِينَ إِمَامًا
Waj'alni lil-muttaqina imama.
"And make me a leader [imam] for the righteous."
Reference: Surah Al-Furqan 25:74

Origin Story

Prophet Sulayman (AS) was given kingdom, wisdom, and understanding of all languages — yet he remained humble. His dua was not for more power, but for the ability to be grateful and to lead righteously. This teaches us that even in positions of strength, we can feel isolated in our responsibilities. The answer to loneliness is not always more people — sometimes it is deeper purpose.

The second dua is part of the beautiful dua of the righteous servants in Surah Al-Furqan: "Our Lord, grant us from among our wives and offspring comfort to our eyes, and make us an example for the righteous." It reminds us that the best companionship is with those who fear Allah.

Has the time not come for those who have believed that their hearts should become humbly submissive at the remembrance of Allah? Surah Al-Hadid 57:16

Reflect & Write

  1. What does loneliness teach me about my relationship with Allah?
  2. How can I turn this solitude into sacred time (khalwah) with my Lord?
  3. What kind of companionship am I truly seeking — quantity or quality?
رَبِّ اشْرَحْ لِي صَدْرِي وَيَسِّرْ لِي أَمْرِي وَاحْلُلْ عُقْدَةً مِنْ لِسَانِي يَفْقَهُوا قَوْلِي
"My Lord, expand for me my chest, ease for me my task, and untie the knot from my tongue that they may understand my words."
Reference: Surah Ta-Ha 20:25-28 (Dua of Prophet Musa)
Chapter Eight
Regret & Guilt
When the past weighs heavy and forgiveness feels far

Introduction

Regret is the heart's recognition that it has strayed. But in Islam, regret is not meant to destroy you — it is meant to return you to Allah. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Allah is more pleased with the repentance of His servant than one of you who finds his lost camel in the desert." (Bukhari & Muslim)

No sin is too great for Allah's forgiveness. No past is too dark for His light. This chapter is your pathway back.

Sayyidul Istighfar (The Master of Forgiveness)

اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي، لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ، وَأَنَا عَلَىٰ عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ، أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ، أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ، وَأَبُوءُ لَكَ بِذَنْبِي، فَاغْفِرْ لِي، فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ
Allahumma anta Rabbi, la ilaha illa anta, khalaqtani wa ana 'abduka, wa ana 'ala 'ahdika wa wa'dika mastata'tu, a'udhu bika min sharri ma sana'tu, abu'u laka bini'matika 'alayya, wa abu'u laka bidhanbi, faghfir li, fa innahu la yaghfiru adhdhunuba illa anta.
"O Allah, You are my Lord, none has the right to be worshipped but You. You created me, and I am Your servant. I abide by Your covenant and promise as best I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your blessings upon me, and I confess my sins to You, so forgive me, for surely none can forgive sins except You."
Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari 6306

The Dua for Deep Regret

اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ الْعَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي
Allahumma innaka 'Afuwwun tuhibbul-'afwa fa'fu 'anni.
"O Allah, You are Pardoning and You love to pardon, so pardon me."
Reference: Tirmidhi (Dua of Aisha RA in the last ten nights of Ramadan)

Origin Story

The first dua is known as Sayyid al-Istighfar (The Master of Repentance). The Prophet ﷺ said: "The master supplication for forgiveness is for a servant to say: 'O Allah, You are my Lord...'" He said: "Whoever says it during the day with firm faith in it and dies that day before evening, he is among the people of Paradise. And whoever says it during the night with firm faith in it and dies before morning, he is among the people of Paradise." (Bukhari)

The second dua was taught by the Prophet ﷺ to Aisha (RA) when she asked: "O Messenger of Allah, if I know which night is Laylatul Qadr, what should I say?" He replied: "Say: O Allah, You are Pardoning and love to pardon, so pardon me." This shows that the highest form of worship in the holiest night is asking for forgiveness and pardon — not material things.

Say, 'O My servants who have transgressed against themselves [by sinning], do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Indeed, Allah forgives all sins. Indeed, it is He who is the Forgiving, the Merciful.' Surah Az-Zumar 39:53

Reflect & Write

  1. What am I holding onto that I need Allah to forgive? Write it as if confessing to the Most Merciful.
  2. How does the hadith about the lost camel change how I see Allah's forgiveness?
  3. What would my life look like if I truly believed I was forgiven?
رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَا أَنْفُسَنَا وَإِنْ لَمْ تَغْفِرْ لَنَا وَتَرْحَمْنَا لَنَكُونَنَّ مِنَ الْخَاسِرِينَ
"Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves, and if You do not forgive us and have mercy upon us, we will surely be among the losers."
Reference: Surah Al-A'raf 7:23 (Dua of Adam and Eve)
Chapter Nine
Love & Compassion
When the heart wants to give and receive pure love

Introduction

Love in Islam is not weakness — it is the very essence of Allah's nature. The Prophet ﷺ said: "You will not enter Paradise until you believe, and you will not believe until you love one another." (Muslim)

This chapter is for love of family, love of friends, love of spouse, love of the Prophet ﷺ, and most importantly, love of Allah. It is for when you want your heart to be soft, forgiving, and full of mercy.

The Dua for a Soft Heart

اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْ فِي قَلْبِي نُورًا، وَفِي لِسَانِي نُورًا، وَاجْعَلْ فِي سَمْعِي نُورًا، وَفِي بَصَرِي نُورًا، وَاجْعَلْ مِنْ خَلْفِي نُورًا، وَمِنْ أَمَامِي نُورًا، وَاجْعَلْ مِنْ فَوْقِي نُورًا، وَمِنْ تَحْتِي نُورًا، اللَّهُمَّ أَعْطِنِي نُورًا
Allahumma ij'al fi qalbi nuran, wa fi lisani nuran, wa ij'al fi sam'i nuran, wa fi basari nuran, wa ij'al min khalfi nuran, wa min amami nuran, wa ij'al min fawqi nuran, wa min tahti nuran, Allahumma a'tini nuran.
"O Allah, place light in my heart, light on my tongue, light in my hearing, light in my sight, light behind me, light before me, light above me, light below me. O Allah, grant me light."
Reference: Sahih Muslim

The Dua for Loved Ones

رَبَّنَا اغْفِرْ لِي وَلِوَالِدَيَّ وَلِلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَوْمَ يَقُومُ الْحِسَابُ
Rabbana ighfir li wa liwalidayya wa lil-mu'minina yawma yaqumul-hisab.
"Our Lord, forgive me and my parents and the believers the Day the account is established."
Reference: Surah Ibrahim 14:41

Origin Story

The first dua is one of the most comprehensive prayers for spiritual illumination. The Prophet ﷺ taught us to ask for light in every faculty — heart, tongue, ears, eyes, and surroundings. Why? Because love without light becomes attachment. Compassion without light becomes burnout. True love is seeing others through Allah's light.

The second dua was made by Prophet Ibrahim (AS) — the Khalilullah (Friend of Allah). Even in his highest spiritual station, his love manifested as concern for his parents and all believers. This teaches us that the highest love is not romantic or selfish — it is the love that prays for others' forgiveness.

And of His signs is that He created for you from yourselves mates that you may find tranquility in them; and He placed between you affection and mercy. Indeed in that are signs for a people who give thought. Surah Ar-Rum 30:21

Reflect & Write

  1. Who do I love so deeply that I want to pray for their forgiveness?
  2. How can I bring more 'light' into my relationships today?
  3. What barriers in my heart prevent me from loving purely and compassionately?
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ حُبَّكَ، وَحُبَّ مَنْ يُحِبُّكَ، وَالْعَمَلَ الَّذِي يُبَلِّغُنِي حُبَّكَ
"O Allah, I ask You for Your love, the love of those who love You, and the deeds that will bring me Your love."
Reference: Tirmidhi
Chapter Ten
Patience & Perseverance
When the road is long and the finish line is hidden

Introduction

Patience (sabr) is not passive suffering — it is active endurance with dignity. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Patience is illumination." (Muslim) It is the weapon of the believer, the companion of the prophets, and the key to every door of mercy.

Allah says: "Indeed, the patient will be given their reward without account." (Surah Az-Zumar 39:10). This chapter is for the marathon, not the sprint. For the long nights, the chronic illness, the difficult marriage, the slow dream.

The Dua for Patience

إِنَّا لِلَّهِ وَإِنَّا إِلَيْهِ رَاجِعُونَ، اللَّهُمَّ أْجُرْنِي فِي مُصِيبَتِي وَأَخْلِفْ لِي خَيْرًا مِنْهَا
Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un. Allahumma ujurni fi musibati wa akhlif li khayran minha.
"Indeed we belong to Allah, and indeed to Him we will return. O Allah, reward me for my affliction and replace it with something better."
Reference: Sahih Muslim

The Dua for Strength

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْعَجْزِ وَالْكَسَلِ
Allahumma inni a'udhu bika minal-'ajzi wal-kasal.
"O Allah, I seek refuge in You from helplessness and laziness."
Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari

Origin Story

The first dua was taught by the Prophet ﷺ to a woman whose husband had died. When she said "Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un," she added: "O Allah, reward me for my affliction." The Prophet ﷺ told her: "You must say: O Allah, reward me for my affliction and replace it with something better." She asked: "What could be better than Abu Salamah?" But she said the dua anyway, and Allah blessed her with marriage to the Prophet ﷺ himself — the best of creation.

This teaches us that when we surrender to Allah's decree and ask for replacement (not just removal), Allah gives us what we could never imagine.

And We will surely test you with something of fear and hunger and a loss of wealth and lives and fruits, but give good tidings to the patient, who, when disaster strikes them, say, 'Indeed we belong to Allah, and indeed to Him we will return.' Surah Al-Baqarah 2:155-156

Reflect & Write

  1. What test am I currently enduring that requires patience?
  2. How can I shift from asking 'Why me?' to 'What is Allah teaching me?'
  3. If Allah were to 'replace my affliction with something better,' what might that look like?
رَبَّنَا أَفْرِغْ عَلَيْنَا صَبْرًا وَثَبِّتْ أَقْدَامَنَا وَانْصُرْنَا عَلَى الْقَوْمِ الْكَافِرِينَ
"Our Lord, pour upon us patience and plant firmly our feet and give us victory over the disbelieving people."
Reference: Surah Al-Baqarah 2:250 (Dua of the army of Talut)
